Kerala to Cascades
A journey between two of Earth's great mountain ranges — both shaped by moisture-laden winds, both harboring extraordinary life, both needing protection.
Your Story
From the cardamom-scented hills of Kerala's Western Ghats — where monsoon clouds crash against ancient mountains and elephants walk through tea plantations — to the salmon streams of Issaquah, where Chinook fight their way upstream past the I-90 interchange.

Two biodiversity hotspots, 8,000 miles apart, connected by the same story: mountains that catch rain, forests that hold water, rivers that give life, and the urgent need for people who can map and protect what remains.

GIS is the language that lets you speak for both landscapes.
The Parallels
🌧️ Orographic Rainfall
Both ranges force moisture-laden winds upward, creating extreme rainfall on windward slopes and rain shadows on the leeward side. The Western Ghats intercept the Arabian Sea monsoon; the Cascades intercept Pacific storms.
🧬 Biodiversity Hotspot
Kerala hosts 62% of Western Ghats endemic vertebrates. The Cascades support the last wild salmon runs in the contiguous US, endangered orcas, and recovering wolf and grizzly populations.
💧 Water Towers
Both ranges are "water towers" — the Western Ghats feed every major river in peninsular India, while Cascade snowpack feeds rivers that supply water to millions and support Pacific salmon life cycles.
🌲 Forest Under Pressure
Kerala's forests face deforestation and mining. Washington's old-growth faces wildfire intensified by climate change. Both need the same thing: people who can map, monitor, and protect what remains.
🤝 Indigenous Connection
The Adivasi peoples of the Western Ghats and the Coast Salish and Yakama peoples of the Cascades both hold deep ecological knowledge. Modern conservation increasingly recognizes traditional stewardship.
🌉 Your Bridge
From the cardamom hills of Kerala to the salmon streams of Issaquah — your journey connects these two biodiversity hotspots. GIS is the language that lets you speak for both.
